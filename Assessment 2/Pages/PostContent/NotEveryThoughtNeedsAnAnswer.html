Not Every Thought Needs An Answer

May 13, 2004

Sometimes it's fine letting thoughts wander without an immediate answer.

Some thoughts arrive without any clear purpose they dont come with a problem to solve or a conclusion for them to reach they simply exist be it briefly, quietly and just disappear as easily.

Its tempting in trying to aswer each one analyzing it until it has a conclusion or just makes sense but not every one of them ask for that kind of attention some are just passing through and forcing an answer can bring more than than satisfaction.

I realized that some thoughts are better left unanswered or ignored doing so will lighten you of burdens of trying to answer each one they not permenant they will be gone sooner or later when you ignore them leaving a sense of space than tension, sometimes its better in letting a thought wander around than to be solved.
