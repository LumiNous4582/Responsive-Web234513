Overthinking And Letting Go

May 5, 2005

Overthinking can be a barrier to progress, learning to let go is essential.

Overthinking may be productive at first giving you the illusion of analyzing or replaying a moment and sooner enough clarity will appear but sometimes it just creates noise your thoughts just becomes more hazy and heavier.

Overthinking usually starts with good intentions wanting to understand, wanting to avoid mistakes, making things right somewhere along the way that intention turns into hesitation the mind circling the same ideas over and over not to solve them but being busy with them.

When it comes to that at some point its better to just let it go accept that it wont have an answer yet it does not need to be resolved yet the most productive thing you can do at that point is stepping back and actually try doing something productive than thinking and maybe then it will answer itself.

OVerthinking wants control but letting go begins with trust in yourself and time and clarity will soon show itself soon.
